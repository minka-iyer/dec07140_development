Implementation Rationale

1. Alignment with Client and User Needs

1.1 Client Goals

The Connect Communities Initiative (CCI) aims to foster a sense of digital belonging, wellbeing, and collaboration through accessible online experiences. Its mission is to create a safe digital space where users can share their experiences openly, connect with peers, and alleviate feelings of isolation.

  • Represent and celebrate the identity of the chosen community, recognising and representing diversity rather than relying on stereotypes.
  • Provide clear, accessible information and resources to users.
  • Foster meaningful connections and support within the community.
  • Ensure ethical, inclusive, and culturally sensitive design principles are upheld.

My website meets these needs through a twofold strategy: (a) providing practical information to clients on building their portfolio through completing challenges posted by employers, and (b) creating a space for them to share their experiences and connect with other members of innovating communities through stories.

1.2 User Needs Identified in A3 Design Proposal

Users wanted to showcase their work outside conventional means such as formal portfolios. The Employer Challenges page connects them directly with employers through project-based challenges.

A strong need for easy-to-understand resources without jargon was identified, so the site was arranged intuitively, with concise language and a welcoming space for users to share stories or questions without fear of ‚Äúsounding stupid.‚Äù

The interface was designed with calm colour gradients, golden-ratio spacing, and ambient hover feedback to promote focus. Typography was updated to Antonio for its futuristic yet readable form and Lexend for clarity and approachability.

2. Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

2.1 Accessibility Integration

  • WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
  • Alt text for all imagery
  • Sufficient colour contrast (tested with ratio tools)
  • Keyboard and screen-reader compatibility
  • Scalable typography and responsive design

Accessibility was embedded from the ideation phase ‚Äî high contrast ratios and accessible focus states guided visual design, ensuring inclusivity was built into every page rather than retrofitted at the end.

2.2 Ethical Principles

  • Anonymised user input for story submissions
  • No manipulative or ‚Äúdark pattern‚Äù design elements
  • Consent for data collection and storage
  • Inclusive imagery representing multiple genders and cultures

These measures ensure that the platform prioritises wellbeing and self-agency, aligning with ethical principles of transparency and respect.

3. Variations from A3 Design Proposal

3.1 Identified Changes

  • Primary font changed from Michroma to Antonio for improved legibility.
  • Animations were simplified to improve browser performance.
  • Hero image on the Events page was refined for balance and alignment.

3.2 Justification for Variations

Feedback indicated that Michroma could be difficult to read on smaller screens. Antonio maintains the futuristic style while improving accessibility. Simplified animations reduced visual noise and loading time without losing the overall retro-futurist aesthetic.

3.3 Conceptual Continuity

Despite these changes, the site retains full conceptual and emotional consistency with the A3 design vision ‚Äî technology remains human-centred, inviting, and reflective of CCI‚Äôs identity.

4. Challenges and Strategies

4.1 Implementation Challenges

  • Technical: responsive layout, animation timing, API setup and debugging.
  • Collaborative: merging assets and version control.
  • Creative: translating conceptual wellbeing elements into functional UI.

4.2 Strategies to Overcome Challenges

  • Iterative testing and debugging (including assistance from Claude AI).
  • Rapid prototyping in Canva when Figma faced performance issues.
  • Version control via GitHub branches to track design and code changes.
  • Accessibility testing and refinement using browser tools.

4.3 Reflection and Growth

These challenges deepened my ability to translate creative vision into technical implementation. I learned to iterate quickly and make design trade-offs without sacrificing usability, strengthening my confidence in delivering inclusive, community-driven experiences.
