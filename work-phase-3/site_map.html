Site Map

In this exercise, I created a site map based on card sort activity to organise the content and structure of my webpage with the aim to create a user-friendly navigation structure. The accordion shows the website structure. Expand each section to view pages and sub-pages, with notes on user/client needs.

Groups event and forum features that support social cohesion and reduce isolation. Uses Gestalt principles of grouping to organise related elements, making it easier for users to discover opportunities for connection. Anonymous Q&A forums and stories hubs also provide safe spaces where questions and experiences are treated with respect, addressing Value 1: Social Cohesion.

  • Find an event: Helps users discover events that match their interests and filter by location, supporting social connection and reducing isolation.
  • Post an event: Empowers users to contribute and share activities, celebrating community identity and diversity.
  • Event calendar: Provides a clear overview of upcoming activities, reducing cognitive load through familiar calendar mapping.
  • Anonymous Q&A Forum: Safe space for asking questions without judgment, supporting inclusivity and respect.
  • Community Stories Hub: Allows sharing of experiences to strengthen belonging and identity.
  • Interest-based Groups: Connects people with shared passions, fostering meaningful relationships.
  • Member Profiles/About Me: Builds visibility and recognition within the community, supporting stronger peer connections.

Provides quick access to credible, evidence-backed resources recommended by peers and experts. Aligns with user needs for trusted information and WCAG 1.3.2 (meaningful sequence) by ensuring a logical structure. Features such as a Resources Library and Tech Events Calendar reduce cognitive load by mapping to users‚Äô mental models of where to find learning content (Conceptual Models).

Creates opportunities for career advancement through innovative and intuitive platforms such as Employer Challenges, Hackathons, and CV review services. These allow users to showcase their skills outside conventional pathways, increasing visibility to employers. Features like badges and progress tracking also leverage feedback and micro-interactions to motivate engagement and align with Value 2: Enhanced Visibility.

Responds to user needs for meaningful networking beyond traditional events. Features like a Mentor Directory, Portfolio Showcase, and Peer Networking support Value 3: Inclusivity by ensuring diverse representation and accessibility. Visual cues and consistent layouts aid discoverability and conceptual mapping, while booking systems follow WCAG 2.1.1 (keyboard navigability).

Positioned prominently with a welcoming colour scheme, this section helps new users feel supported and confident navigating the site. It avoids technical jargon, provides accessibility options (e.g., text-to-speech, high-contrast mode), and offers a clear feedback channel. This directly supports Project Goal 4 (ethical, inclusive, culturally sensitive design) and aligns with WCAG 3.3.2 (labels & instructions).
